How-to guides

Step-by-step maintenance for your Bullfrog spa

💧
Weekly chemical check
Every 7 days · 15 min
›
1
Remove cover and run jets for 5 minutes before testing — this circulates water for an accurate reading.
2
Dip test strip at elbow depth for 2 seconds, hold flat for 15 seconds before reading.
3
Check: FC (3–5 ppm), pH (7.4–7.6), TA (80–120 ppm), CH (150–250 ppm), CYA (30–50 ppm).
4
Use the Test Strip tab to photograph your strip for specific dosing recommendations.
5
Add chemicals one at a time with jets running. Wait 15 minutes between additions.

Using FROG @ease SmartChlor? Test every 10–14 days instead of weekly.

🔄
Monthly filter clean
Every 30 days · 20 min
›
1
Power off the spa at the control panel before removing filters.
2
Locate and remove the filter cartridge from the equipment bay or jetpak access panel.
3
Rinse with a garden hose at low pressure, working top to bottom between each pleat.
4
Soak in filter cleaner solution for 1 hour, rinse thoroughly, and reinstall.
5
Replace filters every 12–18 months regardless of appearance.

Don't use a pressure washer — it damages filter media and voids the filter warranty.

⚡
Quarterly water change
Every 3–4 months · 2–3 hrs
›
1
Add a line flush product the night before draining to clear biofilm from plumbing.
2
Power off completely. Drain via submersible pump or the drain valve.
3
Wipe the shell with a non-abrasive spa surface cleaner — no household cleaners.
4
Clean and rinse jetpaks while they're accessible.
5
Refill, run jets 10 min, then balance: TA first → pH → sanitizer.

Use a garden hose pre-filter when refilling to remove metals and minerals from day one.

🫧
Shocking the water
Weekly or after heavy use · 10 min
›
1
Shock after every heavy use session (3+ people, 45+ min) and as part of weekly routine.
2
With cover off and jets running, dissolve shock in a bucket of warm spa water first.
3
FROG @ease: use MPS (non-chlorine) shock. Traditional chlorine: use dichlor shock.
4
Leave cover off for 20 minutes after shocking to allow off-gassing.
5
Wait until FC returns to 3–5 ppm before use (usually 30–60 min for MPS shock).

Never mix shock with other chemicals before adding to water. Add separately, 15 minutes apart.

Diagnose and resolve common problems

AppearanceCloudy water
›
Most likely cause
pH or TA out of range, or chlorine depleted. Test your water first — don't dose blindly.
Fix it
1. Balance pH (7.4–7.6) and TA (80–120 ppm). 2. Shock with appropriate shock for your system. 3. Run jets with cover off for 30 min. 4. Still cloudy after 24 hrs? Add clarifier and clean or replace your filter.

Persistent cloudiness after balancing usually means a dirty or worn-out filter. When did you last clean it?

FoamExcessive foaming
›
Most likely cause
Soaps, lotions, or detergent residue from swimwear. Low calcium hardness also causes foam.
Fix it
1. Add a defoamer short-term. 2. Shower before using the spa — most effective prevention. 3. Rinse swimwear without detergent. 4. Check calcium hardness (target: 150–250 ppm). 5. If foam keeps returning, a water change is the real fix.

Quick test: shake a sealed bottle of spa water. Heavy foam that takes 10+ seconds to settle means your chemistry needs attention.

OdorStrong chlorine smell
›
What's actually happening
Counterintuitively, a strong chlorine smell usually means too little free chlorine — not too much. Chloramines (used-up chlorine reacting with body waste) are the culprit.
Fix it
1. Shock the water to break down chloramines. 2. Run jets with cover off for 20–30 min. 3. Retest FC and adjust to 3–5 ppm. 4. Check CYA (30–50 ppm) — high CYA blocks chlorine effectiveness.
AppearanceGreen or discolored water
›
Most likely cause
Algae (green), metals in fill water (green/blue/brown), or a pH crash. Rare in hot tubs but happens with neglected chemistry or a fresh fill.
Fix it
1. Test pH, FC, and metals. 2. If metals present, add a metal sequestrant before anything else. 3. If algae, shock aggressively and brush surfaces. 4. Run filter continuously until clear. 5. No improvement in 48 hrs — drain and refill.

Don't shock if metals are present — it causes staining. Sequestrant first, always.

EquipmentJets have low pressure
›
Most likely cause
Dirty or clogged filter is #1. Air lock after a water change is #2.
Fix it
1. Check and clean the filter — if overdue, this is almost certainly the issue. 2. After a water change, open the air bleed valve near the pump to release trapped air. 3. Check that all jet faces are open — Bullfrog jetpaks have individual flow controls. 4. Inspect for kinked plumbing around the jetpaks.

For pump and equipment issues beyond DIY

EquipmentError code on display
›
Common codes
OH / OHH: Overheat — water above 112°F. Turn off, remove cover, let cool. Check filter flow.

FLO / FL1 / FL2: Flow error — dirty filter or air lock. Clean filter first.

SN / SNSR: Sensor fault — power cycle the spa. Persists? Sensor likely needs replacement.

DR / DRY: Dry fire protection — check water level first.
